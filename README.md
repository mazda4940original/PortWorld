## What if AI could see the world the way we do?

PortWorld is an open source framework that lets anyone connect their Meta glasses to any AI. The AI sees exactly what the wearer sees. It can reply with voice, reasoning, or actions.  

This allows you to link your AI agents, customize them, prompt them, and connect devices like your MCP or Openclaw.  

Behind the scenes, PortWorld connects an iOS app on the Meta glasses to a FastAPI backend. This backend handles video streaming, AI processing, voice replies, and tool control. You set up the prompts and logic. PortWorld handles the rest in real time.

## 🚀 Starting PortWorld and Connecting Your Glasses

1. Make sure your Meta glasses are running the PortWorld iOS app and connected to the same Wi-Fi network as your PC.  
2. Open the PortWorld app on your Windows PC.  
3. The app will automatically detect the eyewear on your local network.  
4. When your glasses appear on screen, select them to connect.  
5. The PC app will open a video stream showing what your glasses see.  
6. You can now interact with the connected AI through voice or customize prompts in the app.  
7. Use the built-in voice feature to speak commands or ask questions, and the AI will respond directly.

## 📱 Using PortWorld with Meta Glasses

The iOS app on your Meta glasses must be installed and running for PortWorld on Windows to work.  

- The app captures your field of view video and streams it to the PC backend.
- Your voice commands route from your glasses to the PC, where AI processes them.
- Responses come back through the PC and play through the glasses’ audio output.
- You can switch AI agents or tools on the fly via the PC app interface.

---

## 🛠 Troubleshooting Tips

- Ensure both your PC and Meta glasses are on the same Wi-Fi network.  
- Restart the PC app and glasses app if connections fail.  
- Check firewall settings on Windows to allow PortWorld app network access.  
- Verify that your microphone and webcam permissions are enabled.  
- If video is slow, reduce the stream quality in the settings.  
- Consult the log window in the PC app for error messages.
